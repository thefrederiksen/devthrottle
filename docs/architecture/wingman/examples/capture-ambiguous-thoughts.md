## Findings

- This capture was hunted as the "ambiguous" example, but Claude actually articulated its
  decision point clearly - what it REALLY demonstrates is the missing SEVERITY tier: nothing
  is blocking, yet there IS a real decision available. A binary needs-you/doesn't lies in
  both directions. -> contract: `urgency: "blocking" | "review" | "fyi"`; the rail treats
  fyi as NOT red.
- A genuinely ambiguous capture (where even the strong model must say "unclear, likely X
  or Y") is STILL WANTED - the D7 feedback loop ("this brief is wrong") is the realistic
  collector for those, since they resist being staged on demand.
