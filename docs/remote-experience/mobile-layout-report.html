CC Director — Mobile Layout, Redesigned for Low Cognitive Load

2026-05-23  .  Screenshots captured live in the browser at mobile size (390x844).
The whole design answers one question in one glance: "do I need to do anything?" A big colour-coded word at the top says WORKING, READY, or NEEDS YOU. When it needs you, the exact decision is the loudest thing on the screen and the box at the bottom says "type your answer here". Everything else — the raw terminal, the event log, the ask box — is hidden behind a tab or a tap. Nothing competes for your attention.

The three states

READY
Idle. Nothing to do. Just the state and a one-line reason; everything else collapsed.
WORKING
Busy. A plain-English summary of what it's doing. "Nothing is needed from you yet."
NEEDS YOU
Waiting on you. The question is loud and first; the composer says "Type your answer here".

Why this is low cognitive load

1. State in one word

A big colour-coded word (green/blue/red) is the first thing you see. You know if you can put the phone away before reading anything else.

2. The decision is the hero

When it needs you, "NEEDS YOU" + the verbatim question sit at the very top, above everything. You can't miss what's being asked.

3. One obvious action

The bottom box changes to "Type your answer here" when waiting. There is exactly one place to respond, not two competing inputs.

4. Detail is opt-in

Supervisor log and "ask the supervisor" are collapsed behind a tap. The raw terminal is its own tab. None of it is on screen unless you ask for it.

5. No jargon

"SUMMARY", not "EXPLAIN / opus - auto". The model name, latency, and tool internals are gone from the user's view.

6. Plain-language summary

Instead of a wall of terminal output, the supervisor's plain-English "what's happened / what it wants" is the body. Readable at a glance on a phone.

How it got here (iterated live)

  • v1 — replaced the status dot with a big colour-coded state word; moved the decision card above the summary; collapsed the log.
  • v2 — collapsed "ask the supervisor" too (so there's one obvious input); made the composer say "Type your answer here" when it needs you.
  • v3 — stripped the jargon: "EXPLAIN / opus - auto" became "SUMMARY / updated just now".

Each version was built, launched, and screenshotted at 390x844 in the browser before moving on.

My take, and what's next

  • I'm confident this is genuinely low cognitive load: the eye lands on one coloured word, and when it matters, on one question with one place to answer. There is nothing else to parse.
  • Next, even lower load: when the question has discrete options ("A or B"), render tap-to-answer buttons so you don't have to type at all. That's the natural follow-on to "NEEDS YOU".
  • And a real notification (push / badge) the moment a session flips to NEEDS YOU, so you don't have to open the app to find out.
